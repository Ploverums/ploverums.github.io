In 1999, the first consumer GPU was invented by Nvidia with their Geforce 256. Today, these GPUs have made such an impact that they are one of the most valued parts of a computer, and are in nearly every one. A GPU chip is made with a certain array of chemicals such as copper, boron, cobalt, & tungsten. The graphics card is in the form of a printed circuit board and made with multiple plastics and alloys. The GPU is silicon layered with tantalum and palladium transistors and capacitors. It is used to power the graphical engines of computers. The product is made at a “fab” aka a factory by many different types of machinery. The gpu chip starts off as a piece of quartz, which is then crystalized and melted by seed crystals. The quartz is pulled into a long cylinder that is cut down into a wafer shape by a diamond saw. The silicon wafer is bathed in hydrogen peroxide & sulfuric acid to clean the wafer. It is then put in deionized water and sulfuric acid to remove any particulate matter. The next thing that happens is that deionized water and hydrofluoric acid is used to remove the oxide layer off the silicon wafer. Finally the wafer is washed by hydrochloric acid, hydrogen peroxide, and deionized water to form the chip. The rest of the GPU is 3D printed. The process by which GPUs are made is primarily electrical engineering. Over the years, GPUs have become much smaller, faster, sleeker, and more power efficient. Throughout the lifespan of the consumer GPU, the main design changes have been in the size and manufacturing process. With integrated graphics they have gotten progressively smaller and with more precise machining in the low nanometers, you can fit more power into a smaller space. These improvements were made to push down the price, and push forward the power of the computing space as a whole. If you have any computer, you most likely have a GPU somewhere in it.
